## Step 1: Make sure that you have port-forwarding enabled on your router and check with your ISP

I didn't know this but apparently many ISPs use this thing called CGNAT (Carrier-Grade Network Address Translation). In the internet everybody is addressed an IP Address, however,  in the entire world there's a limited amount of addresses for an ever-growing population of people using the internet. Therefore, ISPs need to distribute one public address with multiple people. While the up-side is that "YAYY everybody can get the internet" the down-side is that you can't use port-forwarding which is very important if you wanna host your own servers.
